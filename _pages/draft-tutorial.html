[toggle title="Adding a New Page or Post and other Page/Post Fundamentals" variation="teal"][fancy_header3 variation="copper" textColor="#000000"]Post vs. Page[/fancy_header3]

[pullquote2 quotes="true" align="center" variation="copper"]There are very key differences between posts and pages in your WordPress blog.[/pullquote2]

Posts

Posts are entries listed in reverse chronological order on the blog home page or on the posts page if you have set one in (Admin dashboard) Settings > Reading. For DDD we are not useing a blog home page in order to precisely control how the posts feed to a given page. If you have created any sticky posts, those will appear before the other posts.

Posts can be found in the Archives, Categories, Recent Posts, and other widgets configured through the admin dashboard. Your taxonomy is an integral part or organizing posts and making them easy to find for your visitors.  Posts are also displayed in the RSS feed of the blog. You can control how many posts are displayed at a time in the Reading Settings. Most all DDD content is packaged in a NEW POST and then presented through a blog shortcode feed on a PAGE, rather then through a blog page.

[icon type="Clipboard" style="inset_silver" align="left"]NOTE: Because of the way we present a category or categories of posts through pages, using the blog shortcode built into your framework, we will not be designating a BLOG page through Settings>Reading.  Instead, we will create a NEW PAGE, give that page a name, and then use the shortcode generator to configure the feed or posts for that page.

 

Pages

Pages are static and are not listed by date. Instead, pages are organized through the navigation or site map.  Pages do not use tags or categories. An About page is the classic example. Pages can be displayed in the sidebar using the Pages widget (Admin Dashboard) Appearance>Widgets, and using the Custom Menu Feature (Admin Dashboard) Appearance>Menus, pages in the horizontal navigation at the top of the each page on the site. The DDD site menu uses the this custom menu feature.  It is still important to position each page you publish in the page tree system so that a proper site map can be generated and Google will index the site with greater accuracy, which leads to greater search engine optimization.
